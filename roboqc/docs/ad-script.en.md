# RoboQC — Ad Script (English)

**Runtime:** ~80 sec · **Format:** 16:9 (re-cut to 9:16 for social)
**Product:** RoboQC — an NVLabs initiative, part of RomeoFlexVision
**Core idea:** "Don't wait for automation — start manually, and automation
assembles itself from your data."

---

## Scene 1 — Hook: the NPI pain (0:00–0:09)

**Video:** A factory floor during a new-product launch. The first batch. An
operator turns a part under the lamp, hesitates, drops it into a "maybe
defective" box. A pile of uncertain parts grows beside them.
**On-screen:** `NPI stage. First batch. Already chaos.`
**Voiceover (VO):** "A new product. The first parts. Quality isn't stable yet —
and there's no data to control it with."

## Scene 2 — Problem: automation needs data that doesn't exist (0:09–0:24)

**Video:** A manager at a monitor. The screen shows an empty automated-inspection
dashboard: "Not enough data to train a model." Split screen: an expensive AOI
line sits idle / the operator checks everything by hand.
**On-screen:** `Auto-inspection can't run without data. And there is none.`
**VO:** "Automated quality control wants thousands of defect examples. At NPI you
don't have them. So you're stuck: manual is slow, automated has nothing to learn
from."

## Scene 3 — Solution: the RoboQC manual scanner (0:24–0:41)

**Video:** The operator picks up the RoboQC handheld error scanner. They pass it
over a part — the scanner screen highlights the zone, one tap tags the defect.
Fast, confident. Every scan flies to the RoboQC cloud (animation: part → data
point).
**On-screen:** `RoboQC: the handheld error scanner`
**VO:** "RoboQC puts a handheld scanner with NVIDIA Jetson edge AI in the
operator's hand — guidance right where the work happens. Every check isn't just
'pass / fail' — it's a labeled example: photo, zone, defect type. Inspection runs
from day one, and content accumulates at the same time."

## Scene 4 — Content accumulates + synthetic data (0:41–0:58)

**Video:** Real scans on one side; on the other, Omniverse renders synthetic
defects; the streams merge into one dataset, which trains a model (TAO). A
labeled-defect counter climbs — 100… 5,000… 50,000.
**On-screen:** `Few defects at NPI? Synthesize the rest.`
**VO:** "Real defects are scarce at NPI. RoboQC fills the gap with synthetic ones
— in NVIDIA Omniverse. The full NVIDIA computer-vision stack turns a handful of
checks into a training dataset."

## Scene 5 — Scaling: automation is ready (0:58–1:12)

**Video:** The same factory, now in volume production. A RoboQC camera
automatically inspects a stream of parts, catches a defect, rejects it. The
operator now supervises instead of re-checking every part.
**On-screen:** `Hit volume — auto-inspection is already trained`
**VO:** "When you reach volume production, the data is already there. RoboQC's
automated inspection on NVIDIA Metropolis and DeepStream switches on
pre-trained — on your parts, your defects, your line."

## Scene 6 — Logo and CTA (1:12–1:20)

**Video:** A clean frame. The RoboQC logo, with `NVLabs` small underneath.
**On-screen:** `RoboQC — quality control from NPI to scale.`
**VO:** "RoboQC. Start manually. Scale automatically."
**End card:** `RoboQC — an NVLabs initiative · romeoflexvision.com → NVLabs section · Request a demo`

---

## Production notes

- **Tone:** confident, engineering-grade, no overacting.
- **Music:** restrained, building rhythm; climax on Scene 5.
- **Recurring visual device:** turning "a part in hand" into "a data point" —
  repeated in Scenes 3 and 4, linking manual work to automation.
- **Cutdown:** a 30-sec version is assembled from Scenes 1 → 3 → 5 → 6.
- **Voiceover:** this English VO is the investor-facing version; the Russian VO
  uses the same edit.
