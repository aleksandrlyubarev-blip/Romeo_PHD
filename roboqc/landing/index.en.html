RoboQC — quality control from NPI to scale

Start manually. Scale automatically. A handheld error scanner collects data from the first day of a product launch — and by the time you reach volume, automated inspection is already trained on your defects.

Request a demo How it works

The NPI trap

New-product introduction is exactly when quality control matters most — and when the data to run it doesn't exist yet.

Manual inspection doesn't scale

Slow, subjective, and unable to keep up with rising volume. Defects reach the customer in early production.

Auto-inspection has nothing to learn from

CV models need thousands of defect examples. At NPI they physically don't exist — so automation never starts.

How RoboQC works

Manual inspection at NPI becomes the training data for automation at scale.

01

Scan

The operator inspects a part with the handheld scanner. Every check is a labeled example: photo, zone, defect type.

02

Accumulate

The defect library grows on its own, inside normal work — no separate labeling project.

03

Synthesize

Rare defects are synthesized in NVIDIA Omniverse — the NPI data gap is closed.

04

Automate

By the time you reach volume, inline RoboQC cameras inspect the stream automatically.
